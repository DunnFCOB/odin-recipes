Authentic Jewish Latkes

Pan Fried Homemade Latkes

I know what you're thinking, "Oh he's about to go on a long winded spiel about his childhood memories, and it's going to take a lot of scrolling to get to the recipe I came here for." Well I'll spare you the details and just give you a little background on what these even are.

A latke is a traditional Jewish potato pancake made out of, you guessed it, potatoes, instead of the fluffy buttermilk stuff you're probably used to. Growing up, any time we had a family gathering my aunt would make several plate fulls of Latkes and were always worth getting excited for.

They're surprisingly simple, yet if you look online for a recipe all you'll wind up with are burnt, hashbrown-style discs that don't even come close to what I grew up with. So with that out of the way, I'll stop rambling and get to what you really came here for.

Ingredients

  • 4 russet potatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 1 egg
  • 1 Tbsp flour
  • Indeterminate amounts of salt (about 1/2 tsp), pepper (around 1/4 tsp), and garlic powder (1/8-1/4 tsp)
  • Sour cream and/or applesauce for dipping

Steps

  1. Blend the potatoes, onion, and egg in a food processor until the mixture is mostly uniform
  2. Pour the mixture into a bowl
  3. Add in the flour and seasonings
  4. Preheat a frying pan on the stove on medium heat, add oil if necessary to prevent sticking
  5. Fry mixture in the pan using enough to make latkes that are 4-5 inches across
  6. Once the latkes are slightly crispy, remove from the pan and place on a paper towel overtop a plate to soak up any excess oil
  7. Serve with your choice of sour cream and applesauce
